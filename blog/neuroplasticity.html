neuroplasticity & growth mindset

neuroplasticity & how it benefits us

Neuroplasticity refers to the "brain's ability to change and adapt as a result of experience", so during my current learning journey I want to adopt the following principles of neuroplasticity:

  • Use it and Improve it: Driving a specific brain function leads to improving abilities

  • Use it or Lose it: Failure to drive specific brain functions can lead to loss of abilities

  • Repetition Matters: Change needs sufficient repetition

  • Salience Matters: the training must be meaningful in order to cause change

growth mindset and the power of yet

is the idea that your intellect and your abilities are not fixed traits but can be improved depending on how you approach and view challenges and setbacks. People with a growth mindset believe it's good to struggle because with some work, your skills will change and get better over time.

Dr Carol Dweck's Ted Talk showed me there are two ways of looking at a problem that is hard to solve - are you not smart enough to solve it, or have you just not solved it yet? 'The power of yet' is something I am going to adopt into my learning plan. It's really encouraged me to embrace challenges and struggling to solve a problem as opportunities to grow.
